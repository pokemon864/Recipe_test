My Taco Casserole Recipe

Description

Taco Casserole is a delicious and easy-to-make dish that combines the flavors of tacos with the convenience of a casserole. It typically consists of layers of seasoned ground beef, tortillas, cheese, and various toppings such as salsa, sour cream, and guacamole. This dish is perfect for a quick weeknight meal or a casual gathering with friends and family.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 packet taco seasoning mix
  • 1 cup salsa
  • 8 small flour tortillas, cut into strips
  • 2 cups shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup chopped green onions (optional)
  • 1/4 cup sliced black olives (optional)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease a 9x13 inch baking dish.
  2. In a large skillet, cook the ground beef over medium heat until browned. Drain any excess fat.
  3. Add the taco seasoning mix and salsa to the skillet with the ground beef. Stir to combine and cook for an additional 5 minutes.
  4. In the prepared baking dish, layer half of the tortilla strips, followed by half of the beef mixture and half of the shredded cheese. Repeat the layers with the remaining tortilla strips, beef mixture, and cheese.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  6. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Top with sour cream, green onions, and black olives if desired.
